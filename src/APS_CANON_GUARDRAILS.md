---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **non-negotiable conceptual constraints** of the APS framework.

While APS_CANON_REVISION.md governs how canonical content may change, this document defines what **must not be violated**.

> Guardrails ensure that APS remains conceptually coherent as it develops.

---

## 2. Core Principle

> APS is an explanatory framework grounded in **viability-oriented, constraint-closed organisation**.

All definitions, articles, and extensions must remain consistent with this foundation.

---

## 3. Triadic Constraint (Agency–Process–Scale)

The APS triad is not a set of components but an **explanatory projection** of a single organisation.

- Agency, process, and scale are **co-constitutive**
- None is ontologically prior
- None may be treated as an independent causal force

### Prohibited:
- “Agency produces process”
- “Process gives rise to scale”
- Any linear or hierarchical ordering of the triad

---

## 4. Viability-Oriented Organisation

Life is defined as:

> **The organisation of viability-oriented, constraint-closed activity**

Therefore:

- Function must be grounded in **current contribution to viability**
- Purpose must be **immanent**, not externally assigned
- Explanation must track **persistence conditions**

### Prohibited:
- Teleology as external design
- Function defined solely by evolutionary history
- Neutral, non-normative accounts of living organisation

---

## 5. Constraint Closure (Necessary but Not Sufficient)

Constraint closure is required for biological organisation but does not alone define life.

- Closure must be coupled with **active regulation of viability**
- Agency is required for life, not closure alone

### Prohibited:
- Treating closure as sufficient for life
- Extending biological status to non-viability-oriented systems

---

## 6. Normativity (Non-Mental, Intrinsic)

Normativity in APS is:

> **The viability-relative asymmetry enacted by ongoing organisation**

- Not subjective
- Not representational
- Not ethical

### Prohibited:
- Reducing normativity to human judgement
- Treating evaluation as requiring cognition or consciousness

---

## 7. Non-Hierarchical Organisation (No “Levels” Ontology)

APS rejects “levels of organisation” as ontological structure.

- Organisation is **multi-scale and continuous**
- Apparent levels are **epistemic abstractions**

### Prohibited:
- “Higher” vs “lower” biological levels
- Top-down or bottom-up causal primacy
- Hierarchical explanatory models

---

## 8. Organism–Environment Coupling

Organisms are not isolated systems.

- Environment is part of the **functional organisation of viability**
- Coupling is **internally regulated**

### Prohibited:
- Treating the environment as purely external
- Internalist or externalist models of control

---

## 9. Anti-Reductionism Constraint

APS rejects component-first explanations.

- Explanation must be **organisation-centred**
- Components derive meaning from their role in sustaining viability

### Prohibited:
- Gene-centric primacy
- Information-first explanations
- Reduction of organisation to mechanism alone

---

## 10. Evolutionary Constraint

Evolution is:

> **The long-term transformation of viability-oriented organisation**

- Natural selection presupposes organised persistence
- Evolution is not primary; it depends on life

### Prohibited:
- Treating selection as foundational
- Explaining life solely through population dynamics

---

## 11. Language Discipline

Language must reflect APS commitments.

- Avoid reintroducing hierarchy, reductionism, or external teleology
- Prefer **organisation-centred phrasing**

### Prohibited:
- Slippage into standard mechanistic metaphors
- Use of “levels”, “programs”, or “control” without qualification

---

## 12. Summary

APS guardrails ensure that:

- Stability is maintained  
- Revision remains coherent  
- Expansion does not produce conceptual drift  

> APS develops through **disciplined constraint, not unrestricted modification**
