## Overview

The APS framework is organised through three distinct but complementary structures: **glossary entries**, **articles grouped into clusters**, and **research streams**. Each serves a different role in the development and communication of the framework.

## Glossary — Conceptual Precision

The glossary provides the **definitional foundation** of APS.

- Each entry defines a core concept  
- Definitions are canonical and stabilised  
- Terms are interlinked to form a coherent conceptual system  

The glossary ensures that APS maintains **terminological discipline and consistency**.

## Articles and Clusters — Structured Explanation

Articles develop the framework through extended analysis and argument.

- Articles are grouped into **clusters**  
- Clusters represent **conceptual domains** (e.g. cognition, empirical interface, explanatory grammar)  
- This provides the primary structure for exploring the framework  

Clusters organise **what is known** within APS.

## Research Streams — Programmatic Inquiry

Research streams organise **ongoing lines of investigation** that extend across clusters.

- They are **cross-cutting trajectories**, not categories of content  
- A single stream may include multiple articles from different clusters  
- They develop over time as sustained programmes of work  

Research streams organise **what is being developed** within APS.

## Why These Are Distinct

These three structures should not be conflated:

- The **glossary** defines concepts  
- **Clusters** organise explanatory content  
- **Research streams** organise ongoing inquiry  

Together, they form a system in which:

> definitions, explanations, and investigations are clearly distinguished but tightly integrated.

## Key Point

APS organises knowledge through glossary definitions, cluster-based articles, and cross-cutting research streams, ensuring clarity between conceptual foundations, structured explanation, and ongoing inquiry.
