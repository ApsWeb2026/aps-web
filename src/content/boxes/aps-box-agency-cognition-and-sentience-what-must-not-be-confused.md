## Why This Distinction Matters

Biological explanation is destabilised when distinct organisational phenomena are conflated. The most common confusion is the collapse of:

- constraint-closed regulation
- normative evaluation relative to viability
- subjective experience

This collapse leads to claims that all living systems are sentient, or that cognition entails consciousness. APS resolves this by distinguishing the organisational basis of life from the possible emergence of experience.

## The APS Distinction

**Agency** — Viability-oriented, constraint-closed activity through which a system sustains its own persistence.  
**Status:** Universal (defining feature of life)

**Cognition** — Evaluation of environmental differences relative to viability within constraint-closed organisation.  
**Status:** Universal in living systems (in minimal form)

**Sentience / Consciousness** — Subjective experience associated with specific organisational configurations.  
**Status:** Contingent, empirically constrained, not required for life

## What Must Not Be Confused

- **Agency is not experience**  
  Viability-oriented activity sustaining persistence does not imply awareness or feeling.

- **Cognition is not representation**  
  Evaluation relative to viability does not require internal models, symbols, or semantic content.

- **Evaluation is not experience**  
  Normative differentiation between better and worse states for a system does not entail subjectivity.

- **Continuity is not uniformity**  
  The continuity of life across scales does not imply that all organisational features are present at all scales.

## The Organisational Ground

All living systems are organised as:

- viability-oriented
- constraint-closed
- normatively structured

This organisational condition is sufficient for:

- agency
- regulation
- evaluation

It is not sufficient, by itself, to establish:

- sentience
- consciousness

## The APS Position

APS affirms:

- all living systems are agential
- all living systems are normatively organised
- all living systems exhibit cognition as evaluation relative to viability within constraint-closed organisation

That is, living systems are organised as viability-oriented, constraint-closed systems whose continued existence depends on their own activity.

APS does **not** affirm:

- that all living systems are sentient
- that evaluation implies experience
- that consciousness is a universal biological property

Sentience is treated as:

- a distinct organisational development
- requiring independent empirical support
- not a defining feature of life

APS does not treat sentience as a ubiquitous or minimally present property of all living systems.

## Why This Matters for Biology

Maintaining this distinction allows biology to:

- describe living systems without anthropomorphism
- preserve continuity between life and mind without conceptual collapse
- ground explanation in observable organisational features

Without this distinction, biological explanation risks either:

- reducing life to mechanism
- inflating experience beyond its evidential basis

APS avoids both errors by grounding explanation in viability-oriented, constraint-closed organisation.

## Key Point

**Agency and cognition are universal features of viability-oriented, constraint-closed organisation; sentience is not. Confusing these levels collapses organisation into experience and obscures the structure of biological explanation.**
