A system may depend on its environment without being controlled by it.

In APS, agency is diagnosed where environmental dependence is organised from within the system’s own viability constraints, rather than imposed or maintained externally.

Dependence alone is not evidence of agency. What matters is whether the system participates in maintaining the conditions of its own persistence.

**Key Point:** Coupling becomes agency when environmental relations are internally organised in the service of viability.
