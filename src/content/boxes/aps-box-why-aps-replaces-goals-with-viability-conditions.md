\# Why This Matters

Biological systems are described as goal-directed: cells repair damage, organisms acquire resources, and development produces organised form. This language captures real regularities but introduces ambiguity. “Goals” imply internally represented targets, intentions, or foresight—concepts that do not apply to most biological systems.

Without clarification, goal language either imports mentalistic assumptions into biology or expands beyond explanatory constraint.

APS resolves this by replacing “goals” with a strictly biological concept: viability conditions.

---

## The APS Reframing

In APS, living systems are viability-oriented, constraint-closed organisations whose activity is regulated relative to the conditions required for continued persistence.

From this perspective:

- biological systems do not pursue goals  
- they maintain viability conditions through organised activity  

Goal-directedness refers to the regulation of activity relative to viability conditions, not the pursuit of an internal target.

---

## From Goals to Organisation

APS replaces goal language with an organisational account:

- \*\*Goal\*\* → viability condition  
- \*\*Goal pursuit\*\* → constraint-regulated activity  
- \*\*Error\*\* → deviation from viability  
- \*\*Success\*\* → sustained persistence  

This shift preserves explanatory precision while removing ambiguity.

---

## Purpose and Function (Canonical Alignment)

APS clarifies teleological language through its core grammar:

- \*\*Purpose\*\* — the system-level orientation toward continued viability  
- \*\*Function\*\* — the mechanistic operations through which that purpose is realised  

“Goals” are not independent entities. They correspond to the conditions that biological purpose maintains and that function operationalises.

---

## Normativity Without Mentalism

In biological systems, some states sustain persistence while others undermine it. This asymmetry is normative because it makes a difference to viability.

Normativity arises from organisation, not intention or cognition.

Goal-directedness therefore refers to activity structured by the difference between what sustains and what degrades viability.

---

## Why APS Uses Viability Conditions

Replacing goals with viability conditions:

1. \*\*Eliminates mentalism\*\*  
   No reference to intention, representation, or foresight  

2. \*\*Preserves explanatory precision\*\*  
   All terms refer to organisational dynamics  

3. \*\*Maintains conceptual boundaries\*\*  
   Distinguishes biological systems from externally imposed or engineered systems  

---

## Key Point

> \*\*In APS, “goals” are viability conditions—the states that constraint-closed systems maintain through purpose and realise in function.\*\*
