In APS, meaning is not introduced as a separate domain layered on top of biology, but arises from the same viability-oriented organisation that defines life.

Living systems regulate their activity in relation to conditions that affect their persistence. Because these conditions make a difference to viability, they are not simply physical states but become biologically significant. Differences that affect persistence are registered and acted upon within the system’s organisation.

This establishes a continuity from viability to meaning. At the most basic level, a system’s activity is structured by what contributes to or undermines its continued existence. This is the foundation of biological normativity.

Where this organisation becomes more complex, systems can differentiate between alternative conditions, anticipate changes, and adjust their activity accordingly. In such cases, the system’s engagement with its environment acquires increasing structure and depth.
