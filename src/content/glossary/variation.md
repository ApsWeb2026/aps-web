## Variation

**Definition**  
Differences in the structure, behaviour, or organisation of biological systems. In APS, variation refers to the **range of possible modifications generated within or across systems**, only some of which contribute to viability-oriented organisation.

**In Brief**  
*Variation is the production of differences; only some differences become transformation.*

### Conventional framing
Variation is typically understood as genetic or phenotypic differences among individuals within a population, arising from mutation, recombination, and developmental processes. It is treated as the raw material upon which natural selection acts.

### APS reframing
APS distinguishes variation from transformation. Variation includes all forms of difference, whether or not they contribute to persistence. Only those variations that are **integrated into constraint-closed organisation** and support viability count as transformation.

Variation therefore:
- generates a field of possible differences in organisation  
- includes both viable and non-viable modifications  
- provides the conditions under which selection and transformation can occur  

Variation is not inherently adaptive or meaningful; its significance depends on whether it becomes incorporated into the system’s **viability-oriented organisation**.

### Key Point  
**In APS, variation generates differences, but only those integrated into viable organisation become transformation.**
