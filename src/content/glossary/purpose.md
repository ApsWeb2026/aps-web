## Conventional framing
Purpose is traditionally associated with intention, design, or goal-directed behaviour. In biological contexts, this has led either to teleological explanations invoking ends as if they were intended, or to the rejection of purpose altogether in favour of mechanistic accounts.

## APS reframing
APS naturalises purpose by grounding it in viability-oriented organisation rather than intention or design. Purpose refers to the organisation of a system’s activity relative to the conditions required for its persistence.

Living systems continuously regulate their activity in ways that sustain their organisation. This activity is not random but organised relative to conditions that support or undermine continued existence. This organisation is enacted through evaluation: the differential modulation of activity in relation to viability. Purpose names this system-scale organisation of activity. This organisation reflects the intrinsic normativity of living systems, through which some states and activities matter for persistence and others do not.

Purpose is therefore inseparable from biological agency. Agency is the ongoing activity of self-maintenance, while purpose is the normatively structured organisation of that activity relative to viability. The two are analytically distinct but inseparable: agency provides the activity through which a system persists, and purpose specifies how that activity is organised in relation to the conditions that sustain it.

Purpose is a global concept: it describes how the activity of the system as a whole is organised. It does not specify the roles of individual components—that is the role of function—but the overall orientation of activity toward maintaining persistence.

By grounding purpose in constraint-closed, viability-oriented organisation, APS avoids both external teleology and reduction to mechanism. Purpose does not require foresight, design, or representation; it is the organisational condition under which activity is organised in relation to the conditions that sustain the system itself.

This account differs from teleonomic interpretations, which treat purposiveness as an effect of evolutionary history. In APS, purpose is grounded in the ongoing organisation of activity relative to viability.

## Key Point
Purpose is the organisation of a system’s activity relative to the conditions required for its persistence.
