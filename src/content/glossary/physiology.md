**Conventional framing**  
Physiology is traditionally understood as the study of how biological systems function—how organs, tissues, and cells operate to maintain life, often described in mechanistic or systems terms.

**APS reframing**  
In APS, physiology is the present-tense organisation of activity through which a living system sustains its own viability. It is not simply the functioning of parts, but the coordinated regulation of constraint-closed processes that maintain coherence across scales.

Physiology expresses viability-oriented activity in real time. It integrates molecular, cellular, and organismal processes into a continuously modulated system that responds to perturbation, maintains stability, and enables persistence.
