## Species

**Definition**  
A historically stabilised pattern of viability-oriented organisation observed across populations and generations. In APS, a species is not a fundamental biological unit but a **scale-relative description of organised persistence** within evolving lineages. It designates a recognisable continuity in the organisation of living systems over time, rather than an ontologically discrete entity or primary causal agent.

**In Brief**  
*A species is a pattern in the history of organised persistence, not a fundamental unit of life.*

### Conventional framing
In standard biology, species are often treated as basic units of classification and evolution, defined through criteria such as reproductive isolation (Biological Species Concept), diagnosable lineage distinction (Phylogenetic Species Concept), or ecological niche occupation. These definitions vary and frequently conflict, reflecting the difficulty of identifying clear and universal boundaries in biological diversity.

### APS reframing
APS interprets species not as fundamental entities but as **epistemic compressions of underlying organisational continuity**. The primary reality is the persistence of constraint-closed, viability-oriented systems; species arise as **historically stabilised patterns within that persistence**.

Species therefore:
- do not ground biological individuality or agency  
- do not function as primary units of selection  
- do not possess fixed or intrinsic boundaries  

Instead, they provide a **scale-appropriate way of tracking evolutionary continuity**, grouping organisms into recognisable lineages based on shared patterns of organisation across generations.

Because these patterns emerge from ongoing processes of persistence, inheritance, and transformation, species boundaries are expected to be **graded, dynamic, and context-dependent** rather than sharply defined.

### Key Point  
**In APS, species are descriptive patterns of organised persistence across time—not fundamental units of life, agency, or causation.**
