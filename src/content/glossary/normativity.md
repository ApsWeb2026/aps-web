definition: "Normativity (biological normativity) in APS arises from the
  precarious, viability-oriented organisation of living systems as they sustain
  themselves over time. Because a living system must continuously maintain the
  conditions of its own persistence, differences in internal states and
  environmental conditions are not neutral: some sustain viability while others
  undermine it. Biological normativity is therefore the viability-relative
  asymmetry generated by a system’s ongoing organisation, through which certain
  processes, structures, or interactions contribute to persistence while others
  disrupt or degrade it. A process or response is normative insofar as it makes
  a difference to the system’s continued coherence by supporting, restoring, or
  undermining the conditions under which the organisation can endure.
  Normativity is not a value, judgement, or externally imposed standard, and it
  does not require intention, representation, or experience. It is endogenously
  enacted as the organised regulation of activity relative to viability,
  grounding biological regulation, error, success, failure, and adaptive
  response."
inBrief: Biological normativity is the enactment of viability-relative
  differences that make a difference to persistence.
