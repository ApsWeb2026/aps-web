In the APS framework, process is not a secondary category subordinated to substance or structure. Drawing on the tradition of process philosophy ([Rescher, 1996](#ref-rescher-1996)), APS treats processes as ontologically fundamental: systems are not things that undergo processes, but rather processes that give rise to (relatively) stable structures.

This commitment has consequences for how APS handles persistence, identity, and change. A living system persists not because some underlying substance endures, but because its constitutive processes are continuously maintained.
