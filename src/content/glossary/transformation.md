## Transformation

**Definition**  
The modification of inherited organisation through adaptive, developmental, or evolutionary change. In APS, transformation is the **reorganisation of constraint-closed processes** that sustains or extends viability across conditions and generations.

**In Brief**  
*Transformation is variation that becomes integrated into viability-oriented organisation.*

### Conventional framing
Transformation is often treated as the accumulation of changes in traits or gene frequencies over time, typically framed as the outcome of mutation, selection, and genetic drift. It is usually understood as a consequence of evolutionary processes rather than as an organisational phenomenon in its own right.

### APS reframing
APS distinguishes between **mere variation** and **viability-integrated transformation**. Not all change counts as transformation: only those modifications that are **incorporated into the system’s organisation** and contribute to continued persistence qualify.

Transformation therefore:
- reorganises constraint-closed processes in response to conditions  
- integrates change into the system’s viability-oriented organisation  
- links short-term adaptation with long-term evolutionary restructuring  

Through inheritance, such reorganisations can accumulate across generations, producing the enduring modification of viable organisation recognised as evolutionary change.

Transformation occupies the long-term organisational pole of the **Persistence–Inheritance–Transformation triad**, linking adaptive reorganisation with the restructuring of viable organisation across time.

### Key Point  
**In APS, transformation is the integration of change into viability-oriented organisation, enabling the long-term evolution of living systems.**
