**Conventional framing**  
Evolution is commonly defined as change in gene frequencies within populations over time, driven by processes such as natural selection, mutation, and genetic drift. This framework provides powerful tools for modelling change but focuses on statistical patterns rather than the organisation that makes such change possible.  

**APS reframing**  
In APS, evolution is the temporal extension of persistence. It describes how viability-oriented organisation is transformed across generations while maintaining continuity. Evolution does not operate on passive entities but on systems that actively sustain themselves.  

Natural selection and other evolutionary processes presuppose the existence of systems capable of maintaining organised persistence and transmitting that organisation across time. Evolution therefore transforms not only biological structures but the organisational capacities through which systems regulate their own viability.  

**Key Point**  
Evolution is the historical transformation of persistence-sustaining organisation.
