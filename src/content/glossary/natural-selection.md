## Natural Selection

**Definition**  
A process of differential persistence and propagation among variants within populations of living systems. In APS, natural selection operates only within **constraint-closed, viability-oriented organisation**, where differences in structure or behaviour contribute to the continued persistence of the system. It is therefore not a primary generative force, but a **derivative process** that depends on the prior existence of organised biological persistence.

**In Brief**  
*Natural selection filters variation within systems that already sustain organised persistence; it does not generate that organisation.*

### Conventional framing
In standard evolutionary biology, natural selection is often treated as the central causal mechanism of evolution, defined as differential reproductive success arising from heritable variation. It is frequently described as the process by which populations adapt to their environments, sometimes implicitly granting it a creative or explanatory primacy in the origin of biological form and function.

### APS reframing
APS repositions natural selection as **secondary to the organisation of living systems**. Selection can occur only where systems already maintain themselves as **viability-oriented, constraint-closed organisations** capable of generating, sustaining, and transmitting variation across time.

Natural selection therefore:
- presupposes the existence of organised, persisting agents  
- operates on variations in the contribution of traits to system-level viability  
- does not generate organisation, but **modulates which variations persist within it**  

Rather than acting on abstract units such as genes or species, selection operates within **organised systems whose persistence provides the conditions for any differential outcome to matter**.

In this sense, natural selection is best understood as a **differential filtering of viable organisation**, shaping the historical trajectory of systems that are already capable of sustaining themselves.

### Key Point  
**In APS, natural selection is a derivative process that filters variation within viability-oriented organisation—it presupposes, rather than explains, the persistence of living systems.**
