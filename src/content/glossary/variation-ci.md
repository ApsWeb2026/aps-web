## Conventional framing
Variation is typically understood as random genetic mutation and recombination, providing the raw material upon which natural selection acts.

## APS reframing
APS broadens variation to include all sources of organised difference emerging from biological processes. Variation is not purely random but often structured by developmental systems, organism–environment interactions, and constraint-based dynamics.

## Key Point
Variation in APS is generated by the dynamics of living organisation, not restricted to random genetic change.
