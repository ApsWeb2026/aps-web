**Conventional framing**  
Mattering is often understood in subjective or psychological terms, referring to what is significant, meaningful, or valued by a conscious agent. In this sense, it is typically associated with feeling, judgement, or representation, and is not usually treated as a fundamental feature of biological organisation.

**APS reframing**  
In APS, mattering is the naturalised expression of biological normativity in real-time organisation: the way differences make a difference to a living system’s continued viability as enacted through ongoing regulation. Something matters to a system not because it is judged or valued subjectively, but because its effects support, threaten, or restore the system’s constraint-closed organisation.

Mattering is relational and enacted, not intrinsic or representational: a condition, event, or resource matters only insofar as it enters into the system’s evaluative modulation and guides the adjustment of activity in relation to persistence. It therefore expresses how normativity appears at the level of present-centred activity, distinguishing what sustains viability from what undermines it, without requiring cognition, representation, or counterfactual depth.

**Key Point**  
Mattering in APS is the real-time enactment of normativity as the differentiation of conditions according to their effects on viability.
