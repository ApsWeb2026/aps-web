APS is a framework for understanding life as viability-oriented, constraint-closed organisation.

This page provides a concise overview of the framework and its core ideas; for a structured explanation of how those ideas fit together, see Understanding APS — The Structure of the Framework.

## Agency

Agency, in the APS sense, does not require intentions, beliefs, or conscious states. It refers to the capacity of a system to act on its own behalf—to maintain the conditions necessary for its continued existence. This is [biological agency](/glossary/biological-agency/) at its most fundamental.

## Process

APS treats processes as ontologically fundamental. Systems are not things that undergo processes; they are processes that give rise to relatively stable structures. This perspective reshapes how we understand persistence, identity, and change.

## Scale

Scale is not an optional parameter. APS treats scale as a constitutive dimension of both agency and process. What counts as an agent, what counts as stable, and what counts as a process depends on the scale of observation. This is an ontological claim, not merely an epistemic one.

## The Core Structure of APS

APS is built on a small set of tightly integrated concepts that together define what life is and how it must be explained.

At its core, APS defines life as viability-oriented, constraint-closed organisation. Living systems are not identified by traits or components, but by the organised activity through which they sustain their own persistence.

This organisation is maintained through constraint closure: networks of mutually sustaining constraints that preserve the conditions required for continued existence. Because persistence depends on this organisation, not all states are equivalent. Some support viability while others undermine it. This gives rise to biological normativity—the fact that differences matter relative to persistence.

Within this organisation, biological agency is the active regulation of conditions that sustain viability. Living systems do not merely persist; they modulate the organisation that makes persistence possible.

This activity is continuously enacted through process—the dynamic organisation through which constraints are sustained and transformed over time.

At the same time, this organisation is distributed across scale. Biological systems persist through the coordination of activity across spatial and temporal domains, rather than through hierarchical levels.

Agency, process, and scale are co-constitutive: they are analytically distinguishable but inseparable aspects of the same viability-oriented organisation.

Together, these concepts provide a unified account of life, grounding biological explanation in the organisation through which living systems sustain and transform their own existence.

## Key Point
**APS explains life as viability-oriented organisation sustained through constraint closure, enacted through process, regulated through agency, and coordinated across scale.**
