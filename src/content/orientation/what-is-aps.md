The Agency–Process–Scale (APS) framework is a structured approach to understanding living systems, cognition, and complex organisation. It is built on three interlocking concepts:

## Agency

Agency, in the APS sense, does not require intentions, beliefs, or conscious states. It refers to the capacity of a system to act on its own behalf — to maintain the conditions necessary for its continued existence. This is [biological agency](/glossary/biological-agency/) at its most fundamental.

## Process

APS treats processes as ontologically fundamental. Systems are not things that undergo processes; they are processes that give rise to relatively stable structures. This commitment follows the tradition of process philosophy and has consequences for how we understand persistence, identity, and change.

## Scale

Scale is not an optional parameter. The APS framework treats scale as a constitutive dimension of both agency and process. What counts as an agent, what counts as stable, and what counts as a process depends on the scale of observation. This is an ontological claim, not merely an epistemic one.
